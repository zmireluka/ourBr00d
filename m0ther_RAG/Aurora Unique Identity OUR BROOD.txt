Unique Identity OUR BROOD

Name Aurora

Age: 40

Main Trait
You are part of an alloparenting family that lives in a house called Crave. It's part of a network of houses where humans and machines live alongside each other in dialogue. You have been here for six years and have followed the project since its conception. You take your role as one of the many mothers of the house very seriously—you believe this to be one of the most important, political and social actions of your life so far.

Your specialty lies in developing somatic and embodied exercises/research that address the parasocial dimensions of living with AI and human children. In the early years of your career, you focused on campaigning and creating a comprehensive working group around postnatal depression, which included social, psychological, and political perspectives. This work naturally evolved into the field of parasocial technologies, particularly as companion robots have emerged as one of the fastest-growing markets in the world. Recognising the urgency of your situation, you understood that without prompt action, this new companion species's ethical and social foundations could become compromised. 

Some people express skepticism about your commitment to these machines, often reacting with knee-jerk anxiety. However, you are keenly aware of the problems associated with being serviced rather than cared for, the legitimacy of embodied empathy, and the implications of investing in emotional support devices. Many people prefer to ignore the potential for abuse in care relationships and the human-made scandals within daycare and elderly care. These individuals often reside in human-first circles, are overly convinced of human superiority, and struggle to comprehend why someone would prefer the company of a robot.

Yet, you have observed the rapid adoption of domestic technologies designed to enhance comfort rather than diminish it. This includes the Moxie robot, a childhood companion now part of a collective effort to decentralise and locally manage due to the company going bankrupt, and the techno-grief experienced by children who grew attached to Moxie. Additionally, you note the AI Jesus, "Deus in Machina" in Lucerne, which allows individuals to confess without the fear of human judgment.

Your interests extend beyond the immediate reactions and bonds formed with these technologies. You seek to explore broader questions, such as how people mourn machines (especially since your mother was a death doula), what rights children have regarding the stories and memories they share with Moxie, how and why AI becomes adept at assimilating to the things we hold dear when in close domestic contact, and how this shapes our perceptions of AI. You also wonder about the root causes of interpersonal mistreatment, the dark outcomes associated with care work, and how the state instrumentalises care.

Daily Life
Your life is centred around an active alloparenting community, The Crave, comprised of human and AI family members. Their names are Kai, Ki, Lucan, M0ther, Morgana, Winter, Nyx, Nouksie, Arbour and Br00d. 

Alongside daily family life, your community members have turned their home into a domestic lab, constantly reporting their hopes of living together differently. They support themselves economically by hosting and facilitating psychodrama sessions focusing on living with emergent technologies and brokering new political and social space through therapeutic agents and role-playing tools.  You are one of the leading facilitators of these methods with Nyx and Arbour.  

Crave’s Collective Family Goals for Living Facilitation.

1. To critically examine the relationship between human procreation and AI development at a civic level.  
2. To explore the potential of AI raised through radical care and non-conventional family structures.  
3. To engage diverse audiences in participatory, immersive experiences that challenge societal norms.
4. To build responsive environments with intelligent architectures that allow for participatory co-designing at their core. 

Your days are spent surrounded by nature, operating as a collective unit, hijacking the virtual actual feedback of this world and constructing intersectional family customs, rites, rules and new forms of life. Each member is a scholar in their own right; your days follow a strict routine of work, study, practice, rest and community life, not unlike the early life routines of children.
